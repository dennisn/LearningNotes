## Learn how AI works
  - Machine learning: train machine from data
    + Supervised learning: train with **labeled** dataset
    + Unsupervised learning: **un-labeled** dataset --> to identify patterns & structures
    + Reinforced learning: trial-and-error process, guided by a reward system
  - AI limitations:
    + Bias in training data --> need follow up prompt/extra context to guide the model
    + Knowledge cutoff: when the training data end --> need to verify time-sensitive info, or specify your timeframe
    + Factual drift: data become less accurate over time
    + Behavioral drift: behavior change over time as models are updated

## Design great prompts
  - Prompt structure:
    + Persona: what expertise/knowledge you want (e.g. "you are a seasoned team leader with a proven track record of managing large projects")
    + Task: what you want to help with (e.g. "tips for effective meetings")
    + Format: output format (e.g. "format your response as a bullet list")
    + Context: the situation (e.g. "our teams work cross-functionally, and struggling to make clear decisions")
  - Evaluate AI response
    + Accuracy
    + Bias: does output favor one perspective unfairly ?
    + Relevancy: does it stay on topic ?
    + Consistency
  - Tips:
    + Provide examples or reference
    + Use more than text (i.e. documents, images or websites)
    + Improve output through iteration
      - May break up complex tasks into smaller parts (i.e. general direction, then how to apply a specific idea)
    + Add constraints
    + Start new chats if changing topic
  - Most useful: append `please ask me any follow up or clarifying questions before you continue doing work`
