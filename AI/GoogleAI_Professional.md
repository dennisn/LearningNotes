## Learn how AI works
  - Machine learning: train machine from data
    + Supervised learning: train with **labeled** dataset
    + Unsupervised learning: **un-labeled** dataset --> to identify patterns & structures
    + Reinforced learning: trial-and-error process, guided by a reward system
  - AI limitations:
    + Bias in training data --> need follow up prompt/extra context to guide the model
    + Knowledge cutoff: when the training data end --> need to verify time-sensitive info, or specify your timeframe
    + Factual drift: data become less accurate over time
    + Behavioral drift: behavior change over time as models are updated

## Design great prompts
  - Prompt structure:
    + Persona: what expertise/knowledge you want (e.g. "you are a seasoned team leader with a proven track record of managing large projects")
    + Task: what you want to help with (e.g. "tips for effective meetings")
    + Format: output format (e.g. "format your response as a bullet list")
    + Context: the situation (e.g. "our teams work cross-functionally, and struggling to make clear decisions")
  - Evaluate AI response
    + Accuracy
    + Bias: does output favor one perspective unfairly ?
    + Relevancy: does it stay on topic ?
    + Consistency
  - Tips:
    + Provide examples or reference
    + Use more than text (i.e. documents, images or websites)
    + Improve output through iteration
      - May break up complex tasks into smaller parts (i.e. general direction, then how to apply a specific idea)
    + Add constraints
    + Start new chats if changing topic
  - Most useful: append `please ask me any follow up or clarifying questions before you continue doing work`

## Level up your prompts

### Advanced prompt phrases

Specific, precise language to improve quality & depth of AI response

  1. Give AI a process: `First, [action 1]. Second, [action 2]. Finally, [action 3]`
  2. Define the audience & tone: `for an audience of [audience type]. The tone should be [professional ?]`
  3. Set hard constraints: `write a one paragraph summary. Focus exclusively on [topic A] and do not mention [topic B]`
  4. Critique: `Critique this text from the perspective of a [role, e.g. customer]`
  5. Alternative: `Give me 3 different versions of this`
  6. Deepen/expand on the initial input: `Elaborate on point 2` or `more detail & specific examples for point 3`

### Prompt chaining

Using a series of smaller, connected prompts to structure a conversation & break down a big/complex project
Examples:
  - Prompt 1: `I'm going to Paris for 3 days. I like art, historical sites, and parks. Suggest a few well-known places I could visit on my trip`
  - Prompt 2: `Using those locations, create a logical day-by-day itinerary that minimizes travel time.`
  - Prompt 3: `For each day of the itinerary, suggest a few restaurants located near the suggested locations.`
  
==> Result
  - Strategy collaboration: AI as thought partner to critique & refine your strategy
  - Complex problem solving: master the skill of breaking down big project into clear & connected phases
  - Strong output: How layering info. over multiple attempts leads to deeper & more customised results

## Use AI responsibly
  - Human-in-the-loop: as AI only based on data --> no common sense, no context
  - `ACT` responsibly:
    + **A**sk: Is this suitable to use AI (e.g. is data safe to use ? Any policies, regulations)
    + **C**heck: Verify the outputs is correct, factual and appropriate for its intended use (e.g. no bias)
    + **T**ell people when you use AI
  - Best practice for AI use:
    + Track your sources: distinguish between AI-generated content & your own
    + Make your process auditable: lay out your work process
    + Document your verification steps - especially for important deliverables
  - AI agents: AI system that you can delegate complex, multi-steps tasks to. Based components are:
    + Models: the Large Language model behind - the agent engine
    + Permissions: constrains on the agent executions
    + Goals: the borad objective given to the agent
  - AI agents considerations:
    + Risk: impact of a mistake
    + Reversibility: how easily to reverse the action
